Asset is any resource owned or controlled by a business or an economic entity.

Equity is ownership of assets that may have debts or other liabilities attached to them.

Free cash flow (FCF) represents the cash available for the company to repay creditors and pay out dividends and interest to investors.

A liability is something a person or company owes, usually a sum of money. Liabilities are settled over time through the transfer of economic benefits including money, goods, or services.

Net income is gross profit minus all other expenses and costs as well as any other income and revenue sources that are not included in gross income.

Inventory refers to a company's goods and products that are ready to sell, along with the raw materials that are used to produce them. Inventory can be categorized in three different ways, including raw materials, work-in-progress, and finished goods.

Revenue is the total amount of income generated by the sale of goods or services related to the company's primary operations.
